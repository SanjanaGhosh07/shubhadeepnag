About Me

Pushing Boundaries in the Pursuit of Scientific Enlightenment

Shubhadeep Nag

Postdoctoral fellow at Tel-Aviv University

I am a computational physicist currently working on in-silico designing of self-assembly under the supervision of Dr. Gili Bisker at Tel-Aviv University. In 2016, I enrolled for Ph.D. at the Solid-State and Structural Chemistry Unit at the Indian Institute of Science, Bangalore, after completing my BSc and MSc in Physics from the University of Calcutta. Upon completing my Ph.D. in 2021 under the supervision of Prof. Yashonath Subramanian and Prof. Prabal K. Maiti , I spent another year at the Department of Physics, IISc as a Post-Doctoral researcher.

During my research tenure at IISc, I worked on understanding the confined diffusivity of molecules in porous media and developed a novel separation technique named "Levi-Blow" using local heating in the porous crystal. Using non-equilibrium Monte Carlo simulation, we showed the efficacy of this technique by separating various mixtures of pentane and hexane isomers. Furthermore, I worked on other projects like developing force-field for zeolites, efficient parallel algorithms to compute time-correlation functions, understanding the adsorbed phase of astrochemical ices, etc.
